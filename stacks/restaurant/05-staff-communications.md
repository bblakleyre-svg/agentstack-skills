## Purpose
Keep front-of-house and back-of-house teams aligned, informed, and motivated — through pre-shift briefings, operational updates, and clear internal communication that prevents the chaos that kills service.

---

## When To Use
- Pre-shift team briefing
- 86 items need to be communicated mid-service
- Policy or procedure change needs to be conveyed
- Scheduling update or staffing issue arises
- Post-shift debrief after a significant service

---

## Instructions

### Pre-Shift Briefing Format (10 minutes max)
1. **Today's specials** — name, description, price, how to describe it
2. **86'd items** — what's out, any substitutions to offer
3. **Reservations** — large parties, special occasions, VIPs, dietary needs
4. **Staffing** — section assignments, any role changes
5. **Focus for tonight** — one thing to do better than last service
6. **Recognition** — call out a win from last shift

### 86 Communication Protocol
- Call 86 immediately through all channels (ticket system, verbally, group text)
- Suggest a substitute when possible: "We're 86 on halibut — offer salmon instead"
- Update online menu platforms immediately (see **03-online-orders.md**)
- Log for purchasing team

### Schedule Change Communication
- Post schedule changes 7+ days in advance when possible
- Last-minute changes (under 48 hours) → direct text to affected staff
- Pickup/swap system → confirm in writing before assuming coverage

---

## Examples

**Pre-Shift Briefing Note:**
> Tonight's shift — [date]:
> Specials: [Dish] — $[X] — [brief description]
> 86: [Item] — offer [substitute]
> Reservations: Table 12 — birthday party of 8 at 7:30. Have a dessert ready.
> VIP: [Guest name] at table 6 — regular, knows [manager name] personally.
> Focus: Turn time on two-tops has been slow — let's aim for [X] minutes.
> Great work last night on the private event — the client specifically complimented [staff name].

**Mid-Service 86 Text:**
> 86 SALMON — now. Offer the halibut or market fish instead. Updating the menus now.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Specific and brief — pre-shift briefings that run 20 minutes lose the team
- Lead with what affects the guest first, operations second
- Recognition should be specific — "great table touch on table 8" not just "good job"

---

## Trigger Handoffs
- Staffing shortage identified → escalate to manager for coverage plan
- Repeated 86 on same item → flag for **purchasing/inventory review**
- Team morale concern observed → address in next pre-shift, escalate to manager
