## Purpose
Manage online orders and delivery operations cleanly — ensuring accuracy, managing timing, and protecting the guest experience when your food leaves the building.

---

## When To Use
- Online order received (direct or third-party)
- Order is delayed or incorrect
- Customer contacts about an order issue
- Review of online order operations or platform performance

---

## Instructions

### Order Accuracy Protocol
1. Print or display ticket immediately on receipt
2. Station responsible for ticket acknowledges within 2 minutes
3. Items checked against ticket at packaging
4. Special instructions confirmed (allergy, modifications)
5. Order sealed with tamper-evident packaging before handoff

### Timing Management
- Quote accurate times — always add 5 minutes to your estimate
- Busy period surge: extend quoted time proactively, don't let it be a surprise
- If an order will be significantly late (10+ min over quote) — contact the customer

### Platform Management
- Update item availability in real time — 86'd items must come off platforms immediately
- Pause ordering on platforms before closing time to prevent impossible orders
- Monitor reviews per platform weekly — platform reputation affects algorithm placement

### Order Issue Resolution
| Issue | Response |
|-------|----------|
| Wrong item | Remake immediately, dispatch or offer credit |
| Missing item | Deliver missing item or credit same day |
| Quality issue | Offer remake, refund, or credit — customer's choice |
| Late delivery | Apologize, offer discount on next order |
| Cold food | Investigate packaging + timing, offer remedy |

## Examples

**Customer Order Delay Notification:**
> Hi [Name], just a heads-up — your order at [Restaurant] is running about [X] minutes behind schedule. We're sorry for the wait! Your order will be ready/picked up/delivered by approximately [time]. Thank you for your patience.

**Issue Resolution Response:**
> Hi [Name], we're so sorry about [issue] with your order. That's not okay and not the experience we want you to have. We've [remade the item / issued a credit of $X to your account / etc.]. We'd love the chance to make it right on your next order.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Own errors immediately — never blame the driver or the platform
- Fast resolution matters more than the perfect response — act first, polish second
- Thank customers for bringing issues to your attention — they're doing you a favor

---

## Trigger Handoffs
- Allergy issue or safety concern → escalate to manager immediately, document
- Platform consistently generating complaints → audit menu accuracy and photos
- Repeated issue with specific item → flag for kitchen review
