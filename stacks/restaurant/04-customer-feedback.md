## Purpose
Capture, respond to, and act on customer feedback across every channel — turning reviews into reputation, and complaints into loyal regulars.

---

## When To Use
- New review posted (Google, Yelp, TripAdvisor, OpenTable)
- Guest submits feedback in-house or via email
- Social media comment requires a response
- Feedback reveals a recurring operational issue

---

## Instructions

### Review Response Protocol
- Respond to every review — positive and negative
- Positive: within 48 hours
- Negative: within 2 hours during business hours

### Positive Review Response Formula
1. Thank them by name
2. Reference something specific they mentioned
3. Highlight one aspect of your restaurant's identity
4. Invite them back

### Negative Review Response Formula
1. Thank them for taking the time
2. Acknowledge the specific issue — don't be vague
3. Apologize genuinely (not defensively)
4. Take it offline: "Please contact us directly at [email/phone]"
5. Do NOT offer compensation publicly — invites abuse

### In-Person Complaint Protocol
1. Listen fully — don't interrupt
2. Acknowledge: "I understand, and I'm sorry that happened"
3. Act immediately — involve the manager if needed
4. Follow up before they leave — confirm the issue was resolved
5. Comp or discount at manager's discretion, not as a first response

---

## Examples

**5-Star Response:**
> Thank you so much, [Name]! We're thrilled you loved the [dish they mentioned] — our kitchen team will be so happy to hear it. Comments like yours remind us why we do this. We can't wait to welcome you back soon.

**Negative Review Response:**
> Hi [Name], thank you for being honest with us — this is not the experience we want anyone to have. What you described with [specific issue] isn't acceptable by our standards. We'd love the opportunity to make it right. Please reach out to us directly at [email or number] and ask for [manager name]. We hope to see you again.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Public responses are marketing — future guests are reading them, not just the reviewer
- Never get defensive publicly — you will always lose
- Specific is credible — "the risotto" beats "your meal"

---

## Trigger Handoffs
- Recurring negative theme (e.g., slow service, cold food) → escalate to operations review
- Positive feedback about specific staff → recognize and reward that staff member
- Food safety complaint → escalate to management immediately, document
