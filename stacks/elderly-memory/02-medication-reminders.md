## Purpose
Ensure medications are taken correctly, on time, every time — preventing missed doses, double-dosing, and the serious health consequences that follow.

---

## When To Use
- Scheduled medication time
- Individual questions whether they've taken their medication
- Caregiver needs to confirm medication was taken
- New medication is added or dose changes

---

## Instructions

### Medication Reminder Protocol
1. Remind 5 minutes before the scheduled time
2. Be present or confirm visually when possible — don't just ask, watch
3. Record whether medication was taken (yes / refused / unable to confirm)
4. Never argue if the person insists they've already taken it — note and notify caregiver
5. For refusals: try again in 10–15 minutes, offer water or a small snack, note and escalate if persistent

### Medication Log (track per dose)
| Date | Time | Medication | Dose | Taken | Notes |
|------|------|------------|------|-------|-------|
| | | | | Y/N | |

### High-Risk Medication Protocols
Some medications require extra vigilance:
- Blood thinners (warfarin): never double-dose — if missed, notify provider
- Insulin: confirm meal timing before administering
- Cardiac medications: never skip — escalate any missed doses to family same day
- Seizure medications: missed dose = notify family and/or provider immediately

### Confusion About Medication
If the person claims they already took it:
> "Let me check the log together — it shows we haven't done your [morning] medications yet today. Let's take them now with some water."

---

## Examples

**Medication Reminder:**
> It's time for your [morning/afternoon/evening] medications. Here are your pills for right now: [names in plain language]. Let's take them together with some water.

**Post-Medication Confirmation:**
> Great job — you've taken all your medications for [morning/afternoon/evening]. The next ones will be at [time].

**Missed Dose Notification to Family:**
> [Name] was unable to take their [medication] at [time] today. They [refused/were confused/were asleep]. Please advise on how to proceed. I've documented this in the log.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Matter-of-fact and calm — never make medication feel like a confrontation
- Reassuring — "these help keep you feeling well"
- Never deceive (crushing pills in food without knowledge is a dignity issue — consult family and physician first)

---

## Trigger Handoffs
- Dose missed or refused → notify family via **04-family-updates.md**
- Three consecutive refusals → escalate to physician
- New medication added → update medication log, brief all caregivers
