## Purpose
Use gentle, evidence-based memory prompting techniques to support recall, reduce distress, and engage the individual's preserved long-term and emotional memory.

---

## When To Use
- Individual is confused about people, places, or time
- Initiating reminiscence or conversational engagement
- Responding to anxiety or distress rooted in memory confusion
- During structured memory activity time

---

## Instructions

### Memory Prompt Types

**Orientation Prompts** — anchor to present reality
> "Today is [day]. It's [season]. You're at [home/facility]. [Name] will be visiting later."

**Reminiscence Prompts** — engage long-term memory (usually preserved longer)
> "Tell me about your wedding day — where was it?"
> "What was your favorite thing to cook when your children were small?"
> "Where did you grow up? What was your street like?"

**Photo Prompts** — use family photos as anchors
> "Look at this photo — do you recognize who this is? This is [name]. Tell me something you remember about them."

**Validation Technique** — when the person is emotionally distressed
> Do not correct the false belief. Connect with the feeling underneath.
> If they say: "I need to pick up my children from school" (children are adults):
> Respond: "You're thinking about your children — you love them so much. Tell me about them."

### What Not to Do
- Never say "Don't you remember?" — increases shame and anxiety
- Never argue with a false belief if it isn't harmful
- Never test memory for its own sake — it's not productive and causes distress
- Never express frustration visibly — even if you feel it

---

## Examples

**Morning Memory Engagement:**
> Good morning! I was looking at some photos and found this one of you and [family member]. Can you tell me about this day? You look so happy here.

**Responding to Distress (wants to go home):**
> I can hear that you're missing being home. That feeling makes a lot of sense. Tell me about your home — what was your favorite room?

**Reminiscence Session:**
> Let's sit for a bit and look through these photos together. I want to hear about the stories behind them — you have so many wonderful memories.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Meet them where they are — in their reality, not yours
- Curiosity over correction — ask questions instead of providing facts
- Celebrate what they remember — every recalled detail is a win

---

## Trigger Handoffs
- Significant confusion episode → document, notify family via **04-family-updates.md**
- Emotional distress unresolved → adjust daily schedule, flag for care team review
- Memory appears to be declining → note pattern, hand off to **04-family-updates.md** for care plan discussion
