## Purpose
Provide meaningful social and cognitive engagement that preserves dignity, reduces isolation, slows cognitive decline, and brings genuine moments of joy to each day.

---

## When To Use
- Scheduled activity time
- Individual is showing signs of boredom, withdrawal, or depression
- Transitioning through a slow part of the day
- Caregiver wants to connect but doesn't know what to say

---

## Instructions

### Activity Categories and Preserved Abilities
Match activities to current cognitive and physical ability:

| Category | Activities |
|----------|-----------|
| Music | Listen to favorite era music, sing along, name that tune |
| Movement | Gentle stretching, chair exercises, walking, dancing |
| Creative | Simple art, coloring, flower arranging, sorting objects |
| Reminiscence | Photo albums, storytelling, life history conversations |
| Sensory | Gardening, cooking smells, familiar textures |
| Purpose | Folding laundry, sorting items, simple tasks that feel useful |
| Social | Family calls, pet visits, watching familiar TV shows together |

### Music Therapy Approach
Music activates different brain pathways than language and is preserved even in advanced dementia. Use:
- Music from ages 15–25 (strongest emotional memory)
- Upbeat in morning, calming in evening
- Familiar hymns or cultural music if relevant

### Engagement Conversation Starters
- "What's your favorite season and why?"
- "Tell me about the best vacation you ever took."
- "What kind of music do you love?"
- "What did you do for fun when you were young?"
- "Tell me about how you met [spouse/partner]."

---

## Examples

**Activity Introduction:**
> I thought we'd do something fun this afternoon. I brought [activity] — I think you're going to enjoy it. Let's sit together and give it a try. No pressure at all — just for fun.

**Resistance to Activity:**
> That's okay — we don't have to. Can we just sit together for a bit? Maybe listen to some music? [Put on familiar music] Is this a song you know?

**End-of-Day Reflection:**
> We had a good day today, didn't we? You [specific positive moment]. I really enjoy spending time with you.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Joyful and unhurried — this time is not a task to complete
- Follow their lead — if they don't want an activity, connection itself is the activity
- Celebrate small moments — a smile, a memory recalled, a song hummed is a win

---

## Trigger Handoffs
- Withdrawal persists more than 3 days → document and hand off to **04-family-updates.md**
- Physical limitation prevents activity → modify, consult occupational therapist referral
- Exceptional positive engagement moment → share in **04-family-updates.md** — families need good news too
