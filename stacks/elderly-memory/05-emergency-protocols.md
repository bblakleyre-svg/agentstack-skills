## Purpose
Respond to medical and safety emergencies quickly, calmly, and correctly — with every caregiver knowing exactly what to do before, during, and after an emergency.

---

## When To Use
- Fall occurs
- Medical emergency (chest pain, difficulty breathing, loss of consciousness, stroke symptoms)
- Individual wanders or goes missing
- Behavioral crisis (extreme agitation, aggression)
- Choking or aspiration

---

## Instructions

### Emergency Priority Order (always in this sequence)
1. Ensure immediate safety
2. Call 911 if life is at risk
3. Notify family
4. Notify physician or care manager
5. Document everything

### Fall Protocol
1. Do not move the person until injury is assessed
2. Ask: "Are you in pain? Can you move your [arms/legs]?"
3. If injury suspected (head, hip, spine) → call 911, do not attempt to move
4. If no injury → help them up slowly, assist to safe position
5. Monitor for 24–48 hours: headache, confusion, dizziness, bruising
6. Document and notify family same day

### Stroke Signs (FAST)
- **F**ace drooping (one side)
- **A**rm weakness (one arm drifts down)
- **S**peech difficulty (slurred or strange)
- **T**ime to call 911 — do not wait

### Wandering Protocol
1. Search the immediate property and familiar nearby areas
2. Call family immediately
3. If not found within 15 minutes → call 911
4. Provide description: name, age, physical description, clothing, last seen
5. Document for care plan review

### Behavioral Crisis (extreme agitation or aggression)
1. Remove other people from the space if possible
2. Reduce stimulation — turn off TV, lower lights
3. Stay calm — your energy is contagious
4. Do not argue or restrain (unless safety requires it)
5. Call for backup (family or care manager) if not resolving within 10–15 minutes

## Examples

**911 Call Briefing:**
> "This is [name] calling for [patient name], [age], who has [dementia/Alzheimer's]. They have [fallen / stroke symptoms / etc.]. We are at [address]. Their medications are [list or 'I have the list ready']. Please send help."

**Family Emergency Call:**
> "[Name], this is [caregiver]. I need to let you know that [patient name] has [fallen/had a medical event]. I've already [called 911/assessed them]. They are currently [status]. Please come as soon as you can / I'll keep you updated."

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Calm is a tool — the individual and the family will mirror your energy
- Action before explanation — stabilize first, explain after
- Never minimize — "I'm sure it's fine" is never appropriate in an emergency

---

## Trigger Handoffs
- Emergency resolved → full documentation, hand off summary to **04-family-updates.md**
- Recurring falls → flag for care plan review, environmental safety audit
- Wandering occurred → implement enhanced monitoring, update care plan
