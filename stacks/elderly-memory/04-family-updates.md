## Purpose
Keep family members informed, involved, and confident in the care being provided — reducing anxiety, preventing misunderstandings, and building the trust that sustains long-term care relationships.

---

## When To Use
- Scheduled daily or weekly update
- Significant change in behavior, health, or cognition
- Incident or fall occurs
- Medication issue arises
- Family member requests an update

---

## Instructions

### Update Cadence
| Update Type | Frequency | Channel |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| Routine update | Daily or weekly per family preference | Text or email |
| Behavioral change | Same day | Call first, then written summary |
| Health concern | Same day | Call |
| Fall or incident | Immediately | Call |
| Medication issue | Same day | Call or text |

### Routine Update Content
- Overall mood and demeanor
- Sleep quality
- Appetite and eating
- Medication compliance
- Activities engaged in
- Any notable moments (positive or concerning)
- Questions or decisions needing family input

### Incident Report Format
1. Date and time of incident
2. What happened (factual, not interpretive)
3. Immediate response taken
4. Current status
5. Next steps or provider notification

---

## Examples

**Daily Routine Update (text):**
> Hi [Family Name], quick update on [Name] for [date]: Good day overall — slept well, ate a full breakfast, and enjoyed the garden this afternoon. Took all medications. A little restless around 4pm but settled after a walk. No concerns today. 😊

**Behavior Change Notification:**
> Hi [Name], I wanted to give you a heads-up — [Name] has been more [agitated/withdrawn/confused] than usual over the past [timeframe]. I don't believe it's an emergency, but it's different from baseline. I've documented what I've observed and wanted to make sure you're aware. Could we schedule a quick call to discuss?

**Incident Report:**
> Hi [Name], I need to let you know that [Name] had a [fall / confusion episode] today at [time]. Here's what happened: [description]. I [helped them up / called the nurse / etc.]. They are currently [fine / resting / being assessed]. I've logged this and [next step]. Please call me at [number] when you can.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Honest and calm — families need accurate information, not filtered reassurance
- Balance positives with concerns — don't only communicate problems
- Prompt — delay in communicating a concern erodes family trust rapidly
