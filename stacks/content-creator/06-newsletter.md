## Purpose
Build a direct, algorithm-proof relationship with your most loyal audience — turning subscribers into buyers, advocates, and long-term community members.

## When To Use
- Weekly newsletter send day
- Product launch requiring direct audience activation
- Sponsorship asset being included in newsletter
- Engagement has dropped and audience needs re-activation

---

## Instructions

### Newsletter Structure (weekly)
1. **Subject line** — curiosity or personal, under 45 characters
2. **Opening line** — hook in 1 sentence, no preamble
3. **Main piece** — story, insight, or lesson (300–600 words)
4. **This week's content** — links to what you published
5. **One recommendation** — tool, book, resource, or person worth knowing
6. **CTA** — one action: reply, buy, click, share
7. **Sign-off** — personal, brief, consistent

### Subject Line Formulas
- Personal story hook: "I almost quit last Tuesday"
- Specific number: "3 things I stopped doing this year"
- Curiosity gap: "What no one tells you about [topic]"
- Counterintuitive: "Why I turned down $20K"

### Metrics to Track
- Open rate (benchmark: 40%+ is excellent)
- Click rate (benchmark: 3%+ is solid)
- Reply rate (even 1% means you're connecting)
- Unsubscribe rate (above 0.5% per issue → something is off)

---

## Examples

**Opening Line:**
> I made a mistake last week that cost me a brand deal — and I'm glad it happened.

**Main Piece Transition:**
> Here's why it mattered...

**Recommendation Section:**
> **This week I'm obsessed with:** [Tool/Book/Person] — [1-sentence why it's worth their time].

**Reply CTA:**
> Hit reply and tell me: [specific question relevant to the issue's theme]. I read every one.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Write like you're emailing one person, not broadcasting to thousands
- Be specific — stories beat advice, numbers beat generalities
- Consistency over virality — the best newsletters are reliably good, not occasionally great
