## Purpose
Write YouTube scripts that hold attention from first second to last, drive watch time, and convert viewers into subscribers and buyers.

---

## When To Use
- Planning a new YouTube video
- Scripting a talking-head, tutorial, or documentary-style video
- Video has low retention and needs a structural fix

---

## Instructions

### Script Structure
1. **Hook** (0–30 sec) — the promise, the problem, or the provocation. No intros.
2. **Credibility bridge** (30 sec) — why you can talk about this
3. **Content body** — 3–5 main points, each with story → insight → application
4. **Pattern interrupts** — B-roll cues, graphic inserts, or questions every 60–90 seconds
5. **CTA** (last 30 sec) — one action: subscribe, watch next, download something

### Hook Formulas
- Problem: "If you're doing [X], you're losing [Y] and you don't even know it."
- Promise: "In this video I'll show you exactly how I [result] in [timeframe]."
- Curiosity: "Most people think [X]. They're wrong. Here's what actually works."
- Story: "Two years ago I [failure]. Today I [result]. Here's what changed."

### Retention Tactics
- Never say "in this video I'll cover" — just start
- Re-hook every 2 minutes: "here's where it gets interesting"
- Preview payoffs early: "stick around because at [timestamp] I'll show you the exact [thing]"
- End sections with micro-cliffhangers: "but there's a catch..."

---

## Examples

**Hook (tutorial video):**
> Most people set up [X] completely wrong — and they don't find out until they've already lost [consequence]. I made the same mistake for two years. In the next 12 minutes I'll show you exactly how to fix it, and give you the template I now use with every client.

**Pattern Interrupt Cue:**
> [CUT TO B-ROLL: screen recording of the tool] — "Look at what happens when you toggle this setting..."

**Closing CTA:**
> If this helped, the next video to watch is [title] — I'll link it right here. And if you're not subscribed yet, do it now — I post [cadence] on [topic].

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Write for the ear, not the eye — read every script out loud before recording
- Short sentences win on video — maximum 15 words per spoken sentence
- Energy on paper becomes energy on camera — write with momentum
