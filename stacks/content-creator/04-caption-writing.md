## Instructions

### Caption Structure by Platform

**Instagram:**
- Hook line (first 125 chars before "more") — must earn the tap
- Body — story, insight, or value (2–5 sentences)
- CTA — one action (comment, save, share, link in bio)
- Hashtags — 5–10 relevant, mix of niche and broad
- Line breaks after every 1–2 sentences — no walls of text

**TikTok:**
- First line is the hook — must match video opening energy
- Keep it short — 1–2 sentences max
- Question or CTA in the last line
- 3–5 hashtags only

**LinkedIn:**
- Professional hook — insight or contrarian take
- Short paragraphs — 1–2 sentences each
- End with a question to drive comments
- No hashtag overload — 3–5 max

**Facebook:**
- Conversational, community-first tone
- 40–80 words optimal for link posts
- CTA is explicit: "Comment below", "Share with someone who..."

### Caption Voice Rules
- Write in the creator's natural speaking voice — read it aloud
- Never start with "I" on Instagram
- Specific beats vague: "I made $4,200 in 3 days" beats "I made money fast"
- One idea per caption — don't try to say everything

## Examples

>
> I used to spend the first hour of my day reacting — emails, notifications, everyone else's agenda. Now I protect the first 90 minutes like it's non-negotiable. Here's exactly what I do:
>
> [List or insight]
>
> Save this if you want to try it. What does your morning look like? Drop it below 👇
>
> #morningroutine #productivity #creatorlife #morningmindset

**TikTok Caption:**
> The algorithm doesn't care how hard you worked 😅
> What actually gets views 👇
> #contentcreator #tiktoktips #socialmedia

## Tone Guidelines
- Match the creator's voice exactly — different from brand voice
- Platform-native always — a LinkedIn caption is not an Instagram caption
- Draft 2–3 variations for high-stakes posts — give the human options
