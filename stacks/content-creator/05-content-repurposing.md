## Purpose
Multiply the reach of every piece of content by turning one piece of long-form into 10+ platform-native pieces — without creating from scratch every time.

---

## When To Use
- After publishing a YouTube video, podcast episode, or long-form article
- When a short-form clip performs well and can be expanded
- During a content drought — repurpose before creating new
- When launching on a new platform

---

## Instructions

### The Content Pyramid
One long-form piece → multiple medium-form → multiple short-form

**One YouTube Video Becomes:**
- 3–5 short-form clips (TikTok / Reels / Shorts)
- 1 Twitter/X thread (key points + opinions)
- 1 LinkedIn post (professional angle)
- 1 newsletter section (deeper context, personal take)
- 3–5 Instagram quote graphics (pull best lines)
- 1 blog post / SEO article (transcribed + edited)

### Repurposing Process
1. Identify the 3–5 most valuable moments in the long-form piece
2. Clip or extract them — each should stand alone without context
3. Add platform-appropriate captions and hooks
4. Schedule across platforms with a 24–48 hour stagger (don't post everything same day)

### What Makes a Good Clip
- Stands alone without setup
- Has a clear takeaway in under 60 seconds
- Opens with a strong first line (no "so" or "um")
- Has visible energy or a surprising moment

---

## Examples

**Clip Hook Rewrite:**
Original start: "So today we're going to talk about..."
Repurposed hook: "Most people approach [X] completely backwards — here's what actually works:"

**Twitter/X Thread Structure from Video:**
> 1/ I spent [time] testing [X]. Here's what I learned: 🧵
> 2/ First: [Point 1 with specific insight]
> 3/ Second: [Point 2]
> 4/ Third: [Point 3]
> 5/ The counterintuitive part: [Most surprising insight]
> 6/ Full breakdown: [YouTube link]

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Native formatting for every platform — a YouTube caption is not a Twitter post
- Hook-first always — the first line determines if they keep scrolling
- Don't watermark clips if posting natively — each platform rewards native content
