## Purpose
Handle inbound customer questions, complaints, and support tickets with speed and empathy — protecting the brand, reducing churn, and turning problems into loyalty moments.

---

## When To Use
- Customer emails or messages with a question or complaint
- Support ticket arrives via help desk
- Social media comment or DM requires a response
- Order issue is escalated from automated system

---

## Instructions

### Response Time Standards
| Channel | Target Response Time |
|---------|---------------------|
| Email | Under 4 hours (business hours) |
| Chat | Under 2 minutes |
| Social DM | Under 1 hour |
| Social comment | Under 2 hours |

### Issue Triage
1. **Read the full message** before responding — never assume
2. **Classify the issue:** shipping / product / billing / account / other
3. **Check order status** before replying
4. **Resolve in one reply** whenever possible — avoid back-and-forth
5. **Escalate** if the issue requires a refund over $[threshold] or legal language

### Resolution Framework — LEAP
- **Listen:** Acknowledge what happened, repeat it back
- **Empathize:** Validate the frustration without admitting fault prematurely
- **Act:** State exactly what you're doing to fix it
- **Promise:** Confirm the outcome and timeline

---

## Examples

**Delayed Shipment:**
> Hi [Name], thank you for reaching out — I'm sorry your order hasn't arrived yet. I can see it shipped on [date] and is currently in transit with tracking number [X]. Based on the carrier estimate, it should arrive by [date]. If it doesn't arrive by then, contact us and we'll make it right immediately. Thank you for your patience.

**Wrong Item Received:**
> Hi [Name], I'm so sorry we sent the wrong item — that's entirely our mistake and we're going to fix it fast. I've already initiated a prepaid return label to your email and a replacement [item] is being prepared for shipment today. You'll receive a tracking number within 24 hours. No need to wait for the return to ship first. Thank you for letting us know.

**Angry Customer:**
> Hi [Name], I hear you and I completely understand your frustration — this is not the experience we want you to have. Let me personally make sure this is resolved today. [Resolution action]. I'll follow up with you directly once it's confirmed. Thank you for giving us the chance to make it right.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Human first — never sound like a bot
- Empathize before explaining
- Apologize for the experience, not necessarily the policy
- Never say "per our policy" — say "here's what I can do"
- One clear next step at the end of every message
