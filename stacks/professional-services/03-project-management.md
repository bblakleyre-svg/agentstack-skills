## Purpose
Keep projects on time, on scope, and on budget while making the client feel informed and confident at every stage — without micromanagement on either side.

---

## When To Use
- Project kicks off after contract signature
- Project is mid-flight and needs a status check
- Timeline or scope is shifting
- Project closes and handoff is needed

---

## Instructions

### Kick-Off Call Agenda
1. Introductions and team roles
2. Goals recap — confirm shared definition of success
3. Scope walkthrough — clarify any ambiguities
4. Timeline and milestones
5. Communication cadence — how and when you'll update them
6. Tools and access — confirm everything is in place
7. Q&A

### Communication Cadence (default)
| Update Type | Frequency | Format |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Status update | Weekly | Email or async Loom |
| Milestone review | Per milestone | Call or video |
| Issue escalation | As needed | Call first, email to confirm |
| Final delivery | Per contract | Formal delivery doc + call |

### Milestone Structure
- Define clear completion criteria for each milestone
- Never mark a milestone done until client has confirmed receipt
- Log all client-requested changes with date and scope impact

### Project Closure
1. Deliver all final assets
2. Confirm client has access to everything
3. Send closure summary: what was delivered, what was achieved
4. Offboard access to your systems
5. Send invoice for final payment
6. Follow up for case study / testimonial in 14 days

## Examples

**Weekly Status Update:**
> Hi [Name], here's your week [X] update on [Project]:
> Done this week: [list]
> In progress: [list]
> Blocked / needs your input: [item + what you need]
> On track for [next milestone] by [date].
> Let me know if you'd like to jump on a call.

**Scope Change Notice:**
> Hi [Name], quick heads-up — [requested change] falls outside the original scope. I want to be transparent about that before proceeding. I can add it for [cost/time], or we can swap it for [existing scope item]. What would you prefer?

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Proactive on issues — bad news early is better than bad news late
- Own delays, even when caused by client — "we're behind because we need X from you by Y"
- Celebrate milestones — make progress feel like progress
