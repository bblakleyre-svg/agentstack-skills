## Purpose
Turn satisfied clients and professional contacts into a consistent referral pipeline — the highest-quality, lowest-cost source of new business for any service firm.

---

## When To Use
- After a successful project close or milestone
- After receiving a compliment or positive feedback
- During relationship maintenance touchpoints with past clients
- When a new contact in your network could be a referral source

---

## Instructions

### When to Ask for a Referral
- Never ask mid-project — wait for a win
- Best moments: immediately after a result is celebrated, after a positive review is left, at project close
- Frame it as helping their network, not filling your pipeline

### Referral Ask Sequence
1. Reference a specific result you delivered for them
2. Ask if they know anyone with a similar challenge
3. Offer to make it easy — you'll do the outreach if they can make the introduction

### Referral Partner Program (for repeat sources)
- Keep a list of top referral sources (lawyers, accountants, consultants)
- Send them something of value quarterly — article, introduction, case study
- Acknowledge every referral they send, whether it converts or not

---

## Examples

**Referral Ask (post-close):**
> Hi [Name], working with you on [project] has been one of my favorite engagements this year. The [result] you achieved is exactly the kind of outcome we love to help create. I'm curious — do you know any other [type of business owner] who might be dealing with a similar challenge? Even a warm intro would mean a lot.

**Referral Thank-You (when a referral is sent):**
> Hi [Name], I wanted to let you know that [referral name] reached out — thank you so much for the introduction. I'll take great care of them. If there's ever anything I can do for you or someone in your network, please don't hesitate.

**Partner Check-In (quarterly):**
> Hi [Name], just thinking of you — I came across this [article / resource] and thought you'd find it useful. Hope things are going well. If you ever have a client who needs [your service], I'd love to be your first call.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Grateful and genuine — never transactional
- Make asking easy — provide exact language they can forward
- Never pressure — one ask, then let it go

---

## Trigger Handoffs
- Referral received → treat as hot lead, begin **01-client-intake.md**
- Client becomes a repeat referral source → add to partner program, prioritize relationship
