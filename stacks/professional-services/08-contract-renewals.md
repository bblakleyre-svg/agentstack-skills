## Purpose
Retain and expand existing client relationships at renewal — converting satisfied clients into long-term accounts and growing revenue without new client acquisition costs.

---

## When To Use
- 60–90 days before contract end date
- Client signals they want to continue but hasn't formally renewed
- End-of-project discussion includes "what's next?"
- Annual retainer is approaching renewal period

---

## Instructions

### Renewal Timeline
| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 90 days before end | Internal review — should we renew? At what terms? |
| 60 days before end | Open renewal conversation with client |
| 45 days before end | Send renewal proposal |
| 30 days before end | Follow up if not signed |
| 15 days before end | Final push — confirm either way |
| End date | Transition or extension begins |

### Renewal Conversation Framework
1. **Results recap** — lead with what was achieved, in their language
2. **What we learned** — what's working, what to change
3. **What's next** — propose a bigger / better / expanded scope
4. **The ask** — present the renewal proposal

### Expansion Opportunities to Surface
- Increase retainer to cover scope that regularly expands
- Add a new service adjacent to current work
- Introduce additional team members or departments
- Propose a longer contract term for a better rate

---

## Examples

**Renewal Opening Email:**
> Hi [Name], I can't believe we're already approaching the end of our initial engagement — the time has flown. I'd love to set up 30 minutes to talk about what we've accomplished together and what a next phase could look like. Are you open to a call this week?

**Renewal Proposal Intro:**
> Based on [results achieved], we're recommending a renewed engagement focused on [next priority]. In the past [period], we [key achievement]. Here's what Phase 2 looks like: [proposal].

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Approach renewal as a natural next step, not a sales pitch
- Lead with their results — every renewal conversation starts with what they've gained
- If renewal isn't the right fit, say so — long-term reputation beats short-term revenue
