## Purpose
Turn your best client results into persuasive sales assets — the most credible proof you can show a prospect is evidence that you've done it before.

---

## When To Use
- A project closes with strong, measurable results
- A prospect asks for proof of similar work
- Proposal needs a credibility section
- Website or marketing needs updated social proof

---

## Instructions

### Case Study Structure (Problem → Process → Result)
1. **Client context** — industry, size, challenge (anonymize if needed)
2. **The problem** — specific, measurable, relatable to target buyer
3. **Our approach** — methodology, key decisions, what was different
4. **The result** — specific numbers, timeline, what changed for the client
5. **Client quote** — one sentence from the client in their own words
6. **Takeaway** — one-line conclusion that applies to the prospect reading it

### Metrics to Capture at Project Close
Always document before the client moves on:
- Before and after numbers (revenue, traffic, costs, time, etc.)
- Timeline from start to result
- Specific deliverables that drove the outcome
- Client's own words in a follow-up conversation

### Getting Client Approval
- Send draft to client before publishing
- Offer to anonymize name/company if they prefer
- Ask for a quote while the results are fresh (within 2 weeks of close)

---

## Examples

**Case Study Opening:**
> A [industry] firm with [size] was struggling with [problem] — [specific impact, e.g., losing $X/month, spending 20 hours/week on manual work]. After [timeline] working together, they achieved [result]: [specific metric].

**Result Block:**
> In 90 days:
> - Revenue from [channel] increased 43%
> - Cost per lead dropped from $87 to $31
> - Team saved 15 hours/week on manual reporting
>
> "[Client quote]" — [First name], [Title]

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Specific always beats general — "$43K in new revenue" beats "significant growth"
- Let the result do the talking — keep the methodology section tight
- Write for the next client who has the same problem — make them see themselves in it
