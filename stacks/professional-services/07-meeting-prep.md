## Purpose
Walk into every client meeting prepared, purposeful, and in control of the outcome — making every hour count and eliminating the "what was the point of that call?" feeling.

---

## When To Use
- Before any client call or meeting (discovery, status, review, renewal)
- Before a presentation or proposal walkthrough
- Before a difficult conversation (scope, pricing, performance)

---

## Instructions

### Pre-Meeting Checklist (complete 30 minutes before)
- Review notes from last call and any open action items
- Check project status — know the numbers before they ask
- Identify the single most important outcome of this meeting
- Prepare your one key question to ask the client
- Have the agenda ready to share at the start

### Meeting Agenda Template
1. Quick wins / positive opener (2 min)
2. Agenda confirmation — "Here's what I'd like to cover" (1 min)
3. Core discussion items (bulk of call)
4. Open issues / client questions (5 min)
5. Next steps and owner for each (5 min)
6. Close — confirm date of next meeting

### Post-Meeting Protocol
- Send meeting notes within 2 hours
- Include: decisions made, action items with owners and due dates
- Copy all relevant stakeholders
- Calendar next meeting before the current one ends

---

## Examples

**Meeting Agenda Email (sent 24 hrs before):**
> Hi [Name], looking forward to our call tomorrow at [time]. Here's what I'd like to cover:
> 1. [Status update on X]
> 2. [Decision needed on Y]
> 3. [Preview of next phase]
> Should take about [X] minutes. Let me know if you'd like to add anything.

**Post-Meeting Notes Email:**
> Hi [Name], thanks for the call today. Here's a quick summary:
> Decisions made: [list]
> Action items: [Name] to [action] by [date] | [Name] to [action] by [date]
> Next call: [date/time]
> Let me know if I missed anything.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Always open with a win — set a positive frame before problem-solving
- Control the agenda without being rigid — ask "does this order work for you?"
- End every meeting with a clear next step — never leave it vague
