## Purpose
Capture all information needed to scope, price, and onboard a new client — eliminating back-and-forth and creating a professional first impression that justifies premium rates.

---

## When To Use
- Prospect books a discovery or sales call
- Inbound inquiry needs to be qualified before a proposal is written
- New client has signed and needs to be formally onboarded

---

## Instructions

### Pre-Call Intake Form (send before discovery call)
Collect: full name, business name, website, industry, team size, specific challenge or goal, timeline, budget range (optional), how they found you, any previous work with similar providers.

### Discovery Call Structure
1. **Rapport** (5 min) — let them talk, listen more than you speak
2. **Current state** (10 min) — "Walk me through where things are today"
3. **Desired state** (10 min) — "What does success look like in 90 days?"
4. **Gap and pain** (5 min) — "What's stopped you from getting there?"
5. **Fit check** (5 min) — "Here's how we typically work — does that align?"
6. **Next step** (5 min) — proposal timeline, decision process, stakeholders

### Onboarding Checklist (post-signature)
- Welcome email with project timeline
- Kick-off call scheduled
- Access and logins collected
- Kick-off agenda sent 48 hours in advance
- Slack/comms channel created
- Billing set up and confirmed

---

## Examples

**Post-Discovery Follow-Up Email:**
> Hi [Name], great talking with you today. Based on our conversation, I have a clear picture of what you're trying to accomplish and I'm confident we can help. I'll have a proposal to you by [date]. In the meantime, feel free to reach out with any questions.

**Welcome Email (post-signature):**
> Welcome aboard, [Name]! We're genuinely excited to work with you. Here's what happens next: [timeline]. Our kick-off call is scheduled for [date/time]. Please come ready to share access to [specific tools/accounts]. Looking forward to getting started.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Authoritative but collaborative — you're the expert, they're the partner
- Ask one question at a time on discovery calls — silence is productive
- Never rush the intake — missed information at this stage costs margin later
