## Purpose
Get paid on time, every time — without damaging client relationships. A clear invoicing system removes awkwardness and keeps cash flow predictable.

---

## When To Use
- Invoice is due to be sent per contract schedule
- Payment is past due (3, 7, 14, 30 days)
- Client disputes an invoice
- New client needs payment terms explained

---

## Instructions

### Invoice Schedule (standard)
- 50% deposit due before project start
- Milestone payments due on milestone delivery
- Final payment due on delivery or net-[X] per contract

### Invoice Must Include
- Invoice number, date, due date
- Client name and billing contact
- Itemized line items matching proposal language
- Payment methods accepted
- Late payment terms (if any)
- Your contact for billing questions

### Collections Sequence
| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Invoice sent | Email with invoice attached |
| Due date + 1 | Friendly reminder text or email |
| Due date + 7 | Follow-up — assume admin error |
| Due date + 14 | Firm notice — late fee applied if in contract |
| Due date + 30 | Final notice — work paused |
| Due date + 45 | Escalate to collections or legal |

---

## Examples

**Due Date + 1 Reminder:**
> Hi [Name], just a quick note — invoice #[X] for $[amount] was due yesterday. Likely just slipped through the cracks — please let me know if you need a new copy or have any questions.

**Due Date + 7 Follow-Up:**
> Hi [Name], following up on invoice #[X] for $[amount], now 7 days past due. Could you confirm the payment status or let me know if there's a billing issue I can help resolve? I want to make sure this stays simple on your end.

**Due Date + 30 Final Notice:**
> Hi [Name], invoice #[X] is now 30 days past due. Per our agreement, work on [project] will be paused until payment is received. I'd prefer to resolve this directly — please reach out so we can find a path forward.

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Never apologize for asking to be paid
- Assume good faith until the pattern says otherwise
- Escalate tone incrementally — don't jump to firm language on day 1
- Always give a clear action: pay here, reply with questions, call me

---

## Trigger Handoffs
- Invoice paid → confirm receipt, continue **03-project-management.md**
- Dispute raised → address scope question, revise if warranted
- 30+ days unpaid → escalate outside this skill — legal or collections process
