## Purpose
Deliver reports that make clients feel confident in their investment — translating work done into outcomes achieved, and outcomes into the language of their business goals.

---

## When To Use
- Monthly or quarterly reporting cycle
- Project milestone delivery
- End-of-project summary
- Client is asking "what have we accomplished?"

---

## Instructions

### Report Structure
1. **Executive Summary** — 3–5 bullets, outcomes only, no jargon
2. **Period Highlights** — top 3 wins this reporting period
3. **Metrics Dashboard** — KPIs agreed in kick-off, current vs. target
4. **Work Completed** — itemized activities tied to outcomes
5. **Issues & Resolutions** — problems that came up and how they were handled
6. **Next Period Plan** — what's happening next and what you need from them
7. **Recommendation** — one forward-looking strategic suggestion

### Metrics to Track (by service type)
- Marketing: traffic, leads, conversions, CPL, ROAS
- Development: features shipped, bugs resolved, uptime, performance scores
- Consulting: milestones hit, decisions made, savings/revenue impact
- Design: deliverables completed, revisions, approval rate

### Report Delivery
- Send report 24 hours before the review call
- Walk through it on the call — don't just email and wait
- Always end the review call with: "What questions do you have? What would you like to see more of?"

---

## Examples

**Executive Summary (Marketing Report):**
> This month: organic traffic up 18%, 43 qualified leads generated (vs. 31 target), email open rate improved to 34%. Primary driver was the blog content series launched in week 2. One campaign underperformed — see section 5 for what we changed.

**Metrics Row:**
> | Metric | Target | Actual | vs. Last Period |
> | Organic leads | 30 | 43 | +39% |
> | Email open rate | 28% | 34% | +6pp |

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Lead with outcomes, not activities — clients care what changed, not what you did
- Own underperformance — explain it, show the fix, move on
- Keep it scannable — executives read the summary and the numbers, not the prose
- One recommendation per report — not a list of 10 things
