## Purpose
Write proposals that win. Every proposal should feel custom, reflect the client's exact language from discovery, and make the decision easy — not overwhelming.

---

## When To Use
- After a qualified discovery call
- When an existing client requests a scope expansion
- When responding to an RFP or competitive bid

---

## Instructions

### Proposal Structure
1. **Executive Summary** — 1 paragraph restating their problem in their words
2. **Our Approach** — methodology, not just deliverables
3. **Scope of Work** — specific, itemized, no ambiguity
4. **Timeline** — phases with milestones and dates
5. **Investment** — tiered options if possible (good / better / best)
6. **Terms** — payment schedule, revision policy, out-of-scope process
7. **About Us** — 3–5 sentences + 2–3 relevant case studies or results
8. **Next Step** — one clear CTA (sign here / schedule a call)

### Tiered Pricing Strategy
Offer 3 options whenever possible:
- **Core** — essential deliverables, lower entry point
- **Standard** — full scope, your recommended option (anchor this)
- **Premium** — full scope + ongoing support or accelerated timeline

Most clients choose the middle. Tiering increases average contract value by 20–40%.

### Proposal Rules
- Mirror the client's language from discovery — use their exact phrases
- Lead with outcomes, not activities ("you'll have X" not "we will do Y")
- Every line item should answer: "why does this matter to the client?"
- Send within 48 hours of discovery call — momentum matters

---

## Examples

**Executive Summary Opening:**
> Based on our conversation, [Company] is facing [specific challenge they described]. You've tried [what they've tried] and need [outcome they stated]. This proposal outlines how we'll [solution approach] to get you to [specific goal] by [timeline].

**Scope Line Item Format:**
> **Website Audit & Strategy Report**
> A comprehensive audit of your current site identifying conversion blockers, SEO gaps, and UX issues — delivered as a prioritized action plan your team can execute immediately.

**Investment Section Intro:**
> We've structured three options based on your goals and timeline. All options include [guarantee or differentiator]. We recommend Standard for your situation — here's why: [1 sentence reason].

---

## Tone Guidelines
- Confident, not tentative — "we will" not "we can try to"
- Client-centric — their name and company appear more than yours
- Specific — every vague word is a red flag to a savvy buyer
- Short executive summary — 150 words max, they'll read that even if nothing else

---

## Trigger Handoffs
- Proposal sent, no response in 3 days → follow up once with "Any questions?"
- Proposal accepted → hand off to **03-project-management.md**
- Price objection received → address with value reframe, not a discount
- Scope needs adjustment → revise and resend same day
